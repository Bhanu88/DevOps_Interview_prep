What is DevOps?

DevOps is the union of people, process, automation and products to enable continuous delivery of value to our end users. The contraction of “Dev” and “Ops” refers to replacing siloed Development and Operations to create multidisciplinary teams that now work together with shared and efficient practices and tools. Essential DevOps practices include agile planning, continuous integration, continuous delivery, and monitoring of applications.

What is Software configuration management and its advantages?
Software configuration management the discipline of ensuring that all platform and assets which a company/project owns are known and always tracked—any future changes to these assets are known and tracked. You can think of configuration management like an always up-to-date inventory for your technology assets, a single source of truth.
The primary components that comprise comprehensive configuration management are:
  • Artifact repository
  • Source code repository
  • Database for Configuration Management
  • Resource limits
  • Package management
  • Security patches

What is Software release management and its advantages?
Release management oversees the stages involved in planning, scheduling, and managing software releases or builds from development and testing to deployment across various environments. The prime benefits of Release Management in DevOps are:
  • Minimizing Risks and Shortening The Time To Market Software Products
  • Strong auditing and Traceability of changes.
  • No downtime
  • Predictability in SDLC Cycle
  • Aligning business & IT goals
  • clear criteria for success

What is ITSM in context of DevOps?
ITSM is a crucial part of building and maintaining a platform for sound DevOps practices.ITSM makes up one of the three major pillars of IT implementation: people, process, and technology.ITSM brings the processes, DevOps brings the automated technology, and they both bring people.So we can say DevOps and ITSM is complementary to each other.
